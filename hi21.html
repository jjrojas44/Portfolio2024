Case Study:

Universal Studios worlds of wounder
Brief

The conceptual sequel to the first Universal Studios video game, "Universal Studios Worlds of Wonder," is a design for an immersive and interactive theme park adventure. This sequel brings players into a virtual recreation of Universal Studios, allowing them to explore famous attractions, engage in park-themed quests, and unlock secrets hidden throughout.

Project

Video Game Design Concept

Role

Solo Design

Time

2 Week Project

Value Added

"Universal Studios Worlds of Wonder" is an immersive theme park video game where players explore a digital Universal Studios, completing park-themed quests and challenges with intuitive, modern design.

Persona
User Insight

Players want to feel the same excitement and thrill of exploring a real-life Universal Studios theme park, but with the added sense of adventure and discovery that only a video game can provide. They crave an experience that goes beyond simply replicating the park, seeking a journey filled with hidden secrets, unique challenges, and interactions with their favorite movie characters—something they can't get in a physical visit. They want to be the heroes of their own park story, where every corner has a surprise and every attraction feels like a personal adventure.

POV statement

Danny Ruiz, a 26-year-old graphic designer and theme park enthusiast living in Orlando, needs a visually stunning and interactive video game that authentically captures the thrill and atmosphere of Universal Studios. As a creative, detail-oriented, and tech-savvy individual, he’s drawn to environments that are both aesthetically impressive and immersive, with a focus on storytelling that mirrors his favorite theme park adventures. Danny wants an experience that lets him relive and explore Universal Studios in new and unexpected ways, while also tapping into his passion for design and creativity, engaging him with challenges that feel rewarding and visually appealing.

How Might We

How might we recreate the excitement of Universal Studios in a digital format that feels just as immersive and thrilling as the real-life theme park? How might we design intuitive game controls and interfaces that make it easy for players of all ages to navigate a virtual theme park? How might we incorporate educational elements about Universal Studios’ history and attractions in a fun and engaging way within the game? How might we include personalization features that allow players to customize their virtual theme park experience to reflect their favorite attractions and interests? How might we balance the challenge and fun factor in the game to ensure it appeals to both dedicated gamers and casual players looking for a lighthearted adventure?

SWOT Analysis
Problem Statement

Universal Studios fans and gamers are seeking a more immersive and engaging way to experience the excitement of the theme park beyond physical visits. Current theme park video games often lack the depth and interactivity that truly capture the unique atmosphere of Universal Studios. Players need a digital experience that not only brings the park's iconic attractions to life but also offers a seamless blend of adventure, storytelling, and exploration, creating a sense of wonder and connection to the brand.
