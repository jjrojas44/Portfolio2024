My Disney Experience redesign

A redesign of the My disney experience app website.

Case Study:

My disney experience app redesign
Project Overview
  • Project Name: My disney experience app Redesign
  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Solo Designer)
  • Tools Used: Figma, Figjam, Photoshop, Illustrator, Google Drive/Docs, Coolors
  • Timeline: 2 Weeks
  • Objective: Redesign the My Disney Experience app to enhance UX with improved navigation, accessibility, and a modernized UI while optimizing performance for faster access to key features.
Value Added

The redesigned My Disney Experience app provides users with a more intuitive interface, faster navigation, and improved accessibility.

Problem Statement

Challenges with the Existing Website:

  • Confusing Booking Process: Users struggle to find ticket options and complete purchases.
  • Overwhelming Navigation: Too many menu items create frustration.
  • Outdated Visual Design: Poor UI hierarchy and inconsistent branding.
  • Lack of Mobile Optimization: The mobile experience is clunky and difficult to use.

Why a Redesign?

  • To provide a modern, streamlined experience that enhances usability and increases conversions.
  • To improve user satisfaction with an intuitive, visually engaging website.
  • To create a more immersive on-brand experience, aligning with Universal Studios' entertainment focus.

Research

Persona

User Insight

Daniel requires a basic and family-oriented solution to organize and personalize his Disney trips because he wants to spend more time making magical memories with others instead of navigating the app.

How Might We

  • Simplify the trip-planning experience by making the appâ€™s navigation more intuitive, reducing frustration for users like Daniel.
  • Optimize the reservation and Genie+ booking system to allow seamless modifications without confusion or unnecessary steps.
  • Deliver real-time updates on wait times, dining availability, and ride statuses in a way that is easy to access and understand.
  • Enhance personalization by offering tailored recommendations based on user preferences, past visits, and real-time park conditions.
  • Create a visually engaging and on-brand Disney experience that feels magical while being functional and efficient.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths Weaknesses
Strong brand recognition of Disney. Complex app features that may overwhelm new users.
Established user base with potential for loyalty. High expectations for seamless functionality.
Ability to integrate real-time park data. Difficulty in personalizing recommendations for all user types.
Familiarity with the Disney theme parks and culture. Potential overload of information for users.
Opportunities Threats
Expansion into offering more personalized experiences. Competition from other trip-planning apps.
Ability to integrate emerging technologies (e.g., AI for personalization). Changes in park operations that affect app functionality.
Real-time updates could enhance user satisfaction. User resistance to new app features.
Potential for greater customer engagement through seamless experiences. Overdependence on technology could alienate some users.

POV Statement

As a family-focused blogger and father of three, Daniel requires a simplified and user-friendly Disney experience planning and management system because he wants to minimize his time spent on the app and maximize the time he can spend making and sharing magical memories with his family.
