Case Study:

My disney experience app redesign
Project Overview
  • Project Name: My disney experience app Redesign
  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Solo Designer)
  • Tools Used: Figma, Figjam, Photoshop, Illustrator, Google Drive/Docs, Coolors
  • Timeline: 2 Weeks
  • Objective: Redesign the My Disney Experience app to enhance UX with improved navigation, accessibility, and a modernized UI while optimizing performance for faster access to key features.
Value Added

The redesigned My Disney Experience app provides users with a more intuitive interface, faster navigation, and improved accessibility.

Problem Statement

The existing Universal Studios app presented multiple usability challenges that hindered the guest experience. It lacked intuitive navigation, modern visual design, and seamless mobile optimization, causing users to struggle with basic actions like booking tickets and accessing park information — leading to frustration and app abandonment during critical trip-planning moments.

Challenges with the Existing APP:

  • Confusing Booking Process: Users struggled to easily locate ticket options, pricing details, and complete purchases, resulting in high cart abandonment.
  • Overwhelming Navigation: An excessive number of menu items with inconsistent labels frustrated guests trying to quickly find key features like maps or dining options.
  • Outdated Visual Design: The app lacked a cohesive design system, resulting in poor UI hierarchy, inconsistent branding, and low user trust in the digital experience.
  • Lack of Mobile Optimization: The mobile experience was clunky, with misaligned layouts, small touch targets, and no mobile-first consideration — causing delays and guest complaints.

Why a Redesign?

  • To provide a modern, streamlined digital experience optimized for on-the-go theme park guests, increasing usability and conversion rates for ticket bookings and reservations.
  • To improve user satisfaction by offering an intuitive, visually engaging, and brand-aligned app that reduces friction in the planning process.
  • To create a seamless, immersive experience that captures the fun, cinematic identity of Universal Studios while improving accessibility and mobile responsiveness.

Research

Persona

User Insight

Daniel's biggest pain point wasn’t a lack of features — it was that everything was buried behind too many menus. I decided early on that any redesign had to surface his top actions (reserve ride, check wait times, book dining) within 2 taps max, even if it meant cutting secondary features from the homepage.

How Might We

  • Simplify the trip-planning experience by refining navigation and reducing friction points for users like Daniel, ensuring ease of use even during peak park hours.
  • Optimize the reservation and Genie+ booking system to allow seamless edits and additions with clear feedback, minimizing errors and guest confusion.
  • Deliver real-time updates on wait times, dining availability, and show schedules through an accessible and unobtrusive notification system.
  • Enhance personalization by using guest preferences, past visit data, and real-time park conditions to curate relevant recommendations.
  • Create a visually engaging and on-brand Disney experience that feels magical, intuitive, and efficient — without sacrificing functionality for aesthetics.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths Weaknesses
Globally recognized Disney brand equity and strong app download base. Overcomplicated feature set that overwhelms first-time users unfamiliar with Disney’s systems.
Established loyalty among frequent park-goers and annual passholders. High guest expectations for flawless performance and magical experiences, raising the margin for error.
Real-time park data integration enabling on-the-fly updates and recommendations. Difficulties tailoring content for diverse user groups (families, solo travelers, teens).
Strong cultural familiarity with the Disney theme park experience and guest priorities. Information overload within the app interface, leading to guest decision fatigue.
Opportunities Threats
Expanding personalization options to offer unique guest journeys and curated experiences. Emerging competition from third-party park-planning tools and crowd-sourced apps.
Leveraging AI and predictive data for dynamic recommendations and itinerary building. Operational changes (ride closures, policy shifts) risking app experience inconsistency.
Improving guest satisfaction with timely, relevant real-time updates. Guest hesitation in adopting new app features, especially for non-tech-savvy visitors.
Driving deeper guest engagement through proactive, helpful app experiences. Risk of over-reliance on digital tools in traditionally in-person, immersive environments.

POV Statement

As a family-focused blogger and father of three, Daniel requires a simple, reliable Disney trip-planning experience because he wants to minimize time spent troubleshooting an app and maximize time creating magical memories with his family.

Affinity Diagram

This affinity diagram revealed that simplifying navigation and emphasizing real-time information were the top priorities for improving guest experience and operational efficiency on the site.

Empathy Map

The empathy map highlighted that guests' biggest frustrations weren’t load times but rather unclear instructions and poor confirmation feedback during bookings and changes, driving my focus on persistent confirmations and clear, actionable UI cues.

Value Proposition Canvas

The value proposition canvas helped clarify that users valued seamless booking, accurate real-time updates, and personalized recommendations most, reinforcing those as central pillars in my redesign strategy.

Card Sorting

I used the card sorting process to study the My Disney Experience app’s overly clustered navigation, then simplified it into a cleaner 7-option menu for Universal — featuring Map, Attractions, Store, Food, Tickets, Lightning Lane, and Profile — making navigation faster, easier, and more intuitive for guests.

UI Style Guide

Wireframe & Design Evolution

sketchs

I began with rough hand sketches to quickly visualize potential layouts and prioritize key guest actions like ride wait times, mobile food ordering, and park maps. This low-stakes step let me experiment with ideas before jumping into wireframes.

Low Fidelity

I translated my sketches into low-fidelity wireframes to quickly map out the layout and user flows without focusing on visuals yet. My main focus here was making sure essential actions — like navigating to ride wait times or mobile ordering — were no more than two taps away at any time.

High Fidelity

Once the layout felt clean and intuitive, I moved to high-fidelity mockups. Here I incorporated Disney’s visual branding — vibrant colors, playful iconography, and clear typography — while still maintaining simplicity and clarity for easy in-park navigation under pressure.
