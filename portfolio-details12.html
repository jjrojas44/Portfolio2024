Case Study:

Hypothetical Sequel Concept for Universal Studios Theme Park Adventure
Project Overview
  • Project Name: Halo Infinite Requiem (Hypothetical Sequel)
  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Solo Designer)
  • Tools Used: Figma,Photoshop,Illustrator,Gameuidatabase,itch.io,kenney.nl,spriters-resource
  • Timeline: 3 Weeks
  • Objective: To create a nostalgic yet fresh visual design that captures the thrill of theme park exploration, cinematic adventure.
Value Added

The game features a nostalgic yet modern gameplay interface that combines retro pixel art with intuitive UI design, which was enhanced through AI-assisted collaboration for faster, cohesive visuals.

Problem Statement

Challenges with Universal Studios Theme Park Adventure:

  • Lack of Engaging Gameplay Loops: Mini-games lacked replayability, progression incentives, and varied difficulty to maintain long-term interest.
  • Outdated Graphics and Controls: Visuals and control schemes no longer reflected the excitement, energy, and storytelling potential of the parks they represented.
  • Limited Attractions and Worlds: Iconic Universal IPs like Jurassic Park and Classic Monsters were underutilized or absent, limiting nostalgic and fan engagement opportunities.
  • Poor User Navigation: Clunky menus and an unintuitive HUD negatively impacted ease of use, especially for younger players or casual users.

Why a Sequel?

  • To reimagine the theme park experience through a charming, retro-inspired pixel-art world that combines nostalgia with modern gameplay accessibility.
  • To deliver simple, satisfying, 2D gameplay loops that prove great games don't require complexity — just thoughtful design and intuitive fun.
  • To create a nostalgic yet fresh experience that resonates with longtime fans while introducing the magic to a new generation.
  • To embrace the resurgence of retro-style gaming, using pixel art as both a visual strategy and a storytelling medium to evoke timeless charm.
  • To craft dynamic, attraction-inspired gameplay loops with layered mechanics that offer challenge and discovery without overwhelming casual players.
  • To build intuitive menu systems and HUDs that complement retro aesthetics while supporting seamless navigation and co-op play.
  • To integrate AI-assisted pixel design tools, maintaining visual consistency, artistic integrity, and development efficiency across characters and worlds.

Research

Persona

User Insight

Alex seeks a gaming experience that recaptures his childhood memories of Universal Studios while offering engaging, family-friendly co-op gameplay to share with his young child. For him, nostalgia isn’t about reliving the past alone — it’s about passing those memories on and making new ones together through playful, accessible games.

How Might We

  • Design a rewarding progression system where players unlock retro collectibles, themed costumes, and concept art through exploration and teamwork.
  • Expand themed worlds and biomes inspired by Universal’s iconic IPs to deliver diverse, explorable environments and interactive rides with hidden secrets.
  • Enhance local and online co-op modes with intuitive controls, simple drop-in play, and shared achievements to create memorable, cooperative experiences for families.

SWOT Analysis

  • Strengths:
    • Nostalgic appeal through retro aesthetics and recognizable park-based world-building.
    • Family-friendly, cooperative gameplay designed to be approachable but rewarding for all skill levels.
    • Rich potential for integrating Universal Studios IPs into a cohesive, fan-favorite adventure.
  • Weaknesses:
    • Risk of being seen as prioritizing style over gameplay depth if mechanics aren’t fully developed.
    • Technical constraints in balancing modern functionality with retro-style limitations.
    • Potentially niche appeal limited to retro enthusiasts and Universal brand fans.
  • Opportunities:
    • Blend classic pixel-art nostalgia with modern polish to broaden the game’s audience beyond retro fans.
    • Partner with Universal IPs (e.g., Jurassic Park, Monsters, Back to the Future) for cross-promotion and themed content drops.
    • Foster a community-driven platform for sharing custom levels, artwork, and Universal-themed mods.
  • Threats:
    • Oversaturation of retro-inspired indie games in the market, increasing competition for attention and shelf space.
    • Balancing accessible controls for kids with enough gameplay depth for adult players like Alex.
    • High expectations from nostalgic fans demanding faithful park and ride adaptations in both gameplay and aesthetic.

POV Statement

Alex, a nostalgia-driven and tech-savvy father, wants to relive his Universal Studios adventures through engaging, cooperative pixel-art gameplay because it allows him to reconnect with his past while creating new theme park memories alongside his child.

Feature Prioritization Matrix

  • Prioritize nostalgic, high-impact features like classic ride missions, retro collectibles, and park-themed biomes to maximize player delight.
  • Balance feasibility with magic—focusing on mechanics that capture the joy of classic arcade games while keeping controls intuitive for families and casual players.
  • Enhance co-op and social opportunities through couch co-op, player photo ops, and celebratory mini-events like fireworks finales after boss fights.
  • Refine core gameplay with retro polish—tighten platforming mechanics, enhance visual clarity, and integrate playful AI behaviors that channel old-school mischief and theme park whimsy.
