Case Study:

Hypothetical Sequel Concept for Universal Studios Theme Park Adventure
Project Overview
  • Project Name: Halo Infinite Requiem (Hypothetical Sequel)
  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Solo Designer)
  • Tools Used: Figma,Photoshop,Illustrator,Gameuidatabase,itch.io,kenney.nl,spriters-resource
  • Timeline: 3 Weeks
  • Objective: To create a nostalgic yet fresh visual design that captures the thrill of theme park exploration, cinematic adventure.
Value Added

The game features a nostalgic yet modern gameplay interface that combines retro pixel art with intuitive UI design, which was enhanced through AI-assisted collaboration for faster, cohesive visuals.

Problem Statement

Challenges with Universal Studios Theme Park Adventure:

  • Lack of Engaging Gameplay Loops: Mini-games were shallow and lacked lasting appeal.
  • Outdated Graphics and Controls: Visuals and mechanics didn’t age well or reflect the excitement of the parks.
  • Limited Attractions and Worlds: Not all iconic Universal IPs were represented or interactive.
  • Poor User Navigation: Menus and HUD were clunky and not intuitive for players.

Why a Sequel?

  • To reimagine the park experience with a charming, stylized pixel-art world using modern design principles.
  • To bring back the magic through simple yet engaging 2D gameplay — proving that fun doesn’t require complexity.
  • To deliver a refreshing return to a retro aesthetic, offering a nostalgic yet modern take that appeals to both OG fans and new players alike.
  • To embrace the comeback of retro-style gaming, where the pixelated look is celebrated for its artistic appeal and timeless charm.
  • To introduce dynamic gameplay loops inspired by Universal’s iconic attractions, layered with simple but satisfying mechanics.
  • To integrate intuitive menu navigation and HUD systems while preserving the retro feel.
  • To use AI-assisted collaboration in pixel design, ensuring authenticity, visual cohesion, and efficiency in the creative process.

Research

Persona

User Insight

Alex seeks a gaming experience which brings back his childhood memories of Universal Studios while providing substantial family-friendly gameplay that he can play with his young child because for him, nostalgia is not just about reliving the past — it’s about sharing that magic with the next generation.

How Might We

  • Design a charm-filled progression system that rewards exploration, creativity, and teamwork through unlockable retro collectibles, concept art, and themed attractions.
  • Expand themed worlds and biomes with diverse environments inspired by classic Universal rides and movies, encouraging exploration and delighting nostalgic players.
  • Enhance local and online co-op modes to make it easy and fun for families to play together, with intuitive controls, drop-in play, and shared achievements.

SWOT Analysis

  • Strengths:
    • Strong nostalgic appeal through retro aesthetics and theme park-inspired world design.
    • Family-friendly co-op gameplay that’s easy to pick up but rewarding for all ages.
    • Creative integration of Universal Studios IPs, offering rich storytelling and iconic settings.
  • Weaknesses:
    • Risk of being perceived as style-over-substance if gameplay depth isn’t prioritized.
    • Potential hardware limitations for more expansive retro-style visuals or features.
    • Niche appeal may limit reach beyond retro or theme park enthusiast audiences.
  • Opportunities:
    • Blending old-school pixel art charm with modern game polish to attract a wider audience.
    • Collaborations with Universal brands (e.g., Jurassic Park, Classic Monsters) for themed levels and cross-promotion.
    • Creating a community-driven hub for sharing levels, art, or mods inspired by theme park creativity.
  • Threats:
    • Oversaturation in the retro-style indie game market leading to comparisons or overlooked releases.
    • Difficulty balancing simplicity for kids with depth for adult players like Alex.
    • High expectations from nostalgic players demanding authenticity in theme and design.

POV Statement

Alex, a nostalgic tech-savvy dad, wants to relive and share the Universal Studios experience through gameplay because he wants to reconnect with his childhood memories while creating new ones with his own child.

Feature Prioritization Matrix

  • Prioritize magical, high-impact features like ride-style replayable missions, theme park biomes, and whimsical storytelling to delight both new and returning players.
  • Balance feasibility with fun and flair—focus on mechanics that capture classic arcade excitement while staying simple and intuitive for casual players and families.
  • Enhance co-op and social experiences by adding couch co-op, in-game photo ops, and celebratory mini-events like fireworks after boss battles.
  • Refine core mechanics with retro charm—tighten platforming, brighten visuals, and add playful AI behavior that mirrors cartoonish mischief and fun.

Value Proposition Canvas

  • Define the experience as “Gaming meets Theme Park Adventure”—a retro reimagining of Halo where every level feels like a ride and every mission a memory.
  • Boost player joy through collectible skins, nostalgic unlocks, arcade-style scoreboards, and silly cheat codes that bring back the fun of classic gaming.
  • Address common gripes by making the game family-accessible, lowering grind, and focusing on short, punchy, replayable missions perfect for short sessions.
  • Expand community engagement with park-pass style battle passes, fan-voted attractions (new levels), and timed events like “80s Night” or “Cartoon Chaos Week.”
