AniSphere

AniSphere is a unified anime and manga hub that streamlines tracking, reviews, and community interaction through an intuitive, visually engaging web experience.

Case Study:

A Unified Hub for Anime & Manga Fans
Project Overview
  • Project Name: AniSphere
  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Solo Designer)
  • Tools Used: Figma, Photoshop,Photopea,Rive, Google Drive/Docs, Coolors
  • Timeline: 2 Weeks
  • Objective: Create a centralized anime & manga hub that blends watchlists, manga trackers, community forums, ratings, and news into a seamless, modern, fan-first platform.
Value Added

AniSphere unifies anime and manga tracking, reviews, and community features into one platform where fans can log progress, rate titles, discover seasonal releases, and connect with others—creating a fun, streamlined hub that celebrates anime culture.

Problem Statement

Challenges with the Existing Competition:

  • Fragmented Experiences: Anime and manga fans must juggle multiple platforms to track shows, read reviews, and participate in discussions, leading to inconsistent engagement.
  • Limited Social Interaction: Current platforms lack modern, engaging community tools that encourage meaningful connections and active participation among users.
  • Outdated Design & Navigation: Many anime databases feature cluttered layouts and confusing navigation, making it difficult for users to find seasonal releases or manage their libraries efficiently.
  • Lack of Personalization: Users crave tailored recommendations and a personalized experience that reflects their tastes, yet most competitors provide generic or static interfaces.

Why a Redesign?

  • To centralize anime and manga tracking, reviews, ratings, and forums into one cohesive and modern platform.
  • To create a vibrant, social environment where users can engage through discussions, share recommendations, and celebrate fandom culture.
  • To simplify navigation and elevate usability, helping fans discover seasonal releases and trending content effortlessly.
  • To craft a sleek, cohesive design that modernizes the anime/manga experience and stands out visually from existing competitors like MyAnimeList and AniList.

Research

Persona

Aya Tanaka, a 21-year-old college student and devoted anime fan, loves tracking her favorite series, discovering upcoming releases, and joining conversations about character arcs and fan theories. Her frustration with juggling multiple outdated platforms inspired the foundation of AniSphere’s design — shaping its focus on a personalized, modern, and community-driven experience tailored to fans like her.

User Insight

Aya Tanaka constantly seeks a way to keep up with her favorite anime series while connecting with other fans. She wants a platform that combines easy tracking of episodes and chapters, personalized recommendations, and vibrant discussions about characters and storylines. Current platforms feel fragmented and outdated, making it difficult for her to discover new shows and engage in meaningful conversations. Aya desires a modern, integrated experience that keeps everything in one place and adapts to her interests.

UI Inspiration Grid

To define AniSphere’s visual tone, I curated inspiration from anime and manga tracking platforms, community networks, and modern entertainment apps. These references guided the layout structure, color harmony, and typography choices for a bold yet welcoming anime-inspired interface.

Competitive Analysis

I compared MyAnimeList, AniList, Crunchyroll, and Kitsu to evaluate their content organization, social integration, and UI quality. Most platforms excel in data management but fall short in social engagement and visual appeal. AniSphere’s opportunity lies in blending data accuracy with community energy, all under a clean, modern interface.

UI Style Guide

The AniSphere UI blends fun, futuristic energy with clean, accessible design inspired by MyAnimeList, AniList, Crunchyroll, and Kitsu. Bright pop colors balanced with soft whites and grays create a dynamic yet readable interface, while modern typography—Montserrat, Poppins, and Inter—keeps navigation simple, engaging, and effortless.

Competitive Takeaways

  • MyAnimeList (MAL): Offers a massive verified anime and manga database with an active community and deep metadata. However, its outdated UI and poor mobile responsiveness make engagement and discovery feel clunky. There’s strong opportunity to modernize with better UX, personalization, and social integration.
  • AniList: Features a clean, modern interface and strong personalization tools, supported by an active developer community. Yet, its smaller audience and minimal social depth create a less emotionally engaging experience. There’s potential to expand into richer community storytelling and discovery features.
  • Crunchyroll: Industry leader in official anime streaming with high-quality visuals and polished branding. Despite this, it lacks deep social tools and community-building features beyond content consumption. A combined tracking and interaction layer could keep users engaged longer.
  • Kitsu: Known for its modern UI and community-oriented design, Kitsu fosters discussion but suffers from technical instability and limited scalability. Strengthening curation, tagging, and user customization could help it appeal to a broader audience.

Biggest Challenge

  • Existing anime platforms are fragmented—users must switch between apps for tracking, rating, and social discussion.
  • Many sites lack a cohesive design language that captures the vibrancy and emotional depth of anime culture.
  • Social tools are often secondary or buried, leading to inactive forums and limited peer interaction.
  • The challenge was to create a single hub where anime fans like Aya Tanaka could track progress, discover new titles, and join discussions seamlessly on both web and mobile.
  • Through user-driven research and visual exploration, I designed AniSphere as a lively, integrated experience — merging data, discovery, and social interaction in a unified interface that celebrates anime fandom.

Key Insights

  • Anime fans want a centralized, visually engaging hub that brings together tracking, discovery, and social interaction in one clean experience.
  • Users are drawn to platforms that feel alive and expressive, reflecting the creative, emotional spirit of anime culture.
  • While data and ratings are important, community validation and shared opinions are what keep fans returning.
  • Modern, mobile-first design with personalized recommendations builds trust and encourages long-term engagement.
  • By combining structured tracking tools with active community features, AniSphere fills the gap between outdated databases and unstructured fan spaces.

Site Map

The site map features seven main links: Account, Main Page, News, Reviews, Deals, Forums, and Settings. News, Reviews, and Deals each branch into Xbox, PlayStation, Nintendo, and PC sub-links, making navigation fast and frustration-free. This clean, organized structure fixes common issues with cluttered, confusing layouts and helps users find what they want, when they want it.

What I Learned

  • Design Evolution Matters: Comparing my early wireframes to the final version, I realized how much refinement improves clarity and visual hierarchy. The final iteration is significantly more structured and user-friendly.
  • Consistency Enhances Usability: My earlier designs had scattered layouts and inconsistent icon usage. In my final version, I prioritized visual consistency across buttons, filters, and headers to create a smoother user flow.
  • Clarity in Dense Interfaces: The low-fidelity version didn’t fully account for how overwhelming dense data (like multiple game cards or news posts) can be. My final design uses clearer grids, controlled spacing, and better font sizing to make the content digestible.
  • Refining Navigation Through Iteration: I restructured the filter panel and sorting controls in my latest iteration for more intuitive access, learning how micro-adjustments in layout make a big impact on usability.
  • Bringing Visual Identity to Life: The early wireframe lacked a distinct tone. In the final UI, I embedded a unique style—color palette, neon outlines, bold icons, and a space-themed background—to capture the essence of a fun, futuristic gaming platform.
