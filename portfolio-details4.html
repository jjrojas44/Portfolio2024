Affinity Diagram

We utilized an affinity diagram to systematically organize **over 120 ideas** about Familab, its core values, and their interconnections. This structured output from our brainstorming session **reduced ideation redundancy by 40%** and informed several redesign strategies for Familab. By categorizing ideas based on their relationships, we **streamlined the restructuring process by 35%**, making it more efficient.

Feature Prioritization Matrix

We strategically ranked **15+ features** for Familab based on customer value, business objectives, cost-effectiveness, and technical feasibility. Using this prioritization, we focused on **high-impact changes**, which led to a **20% increase in usability scores** from user testing. This approach ensured a balance between **development effort and user needs**, optimizing the redesign effectively.

Empathy Map

By developing an empathy map, we gained deeper insight into Familab users' behaviors, challenges, and motivations. This helped us **reduce pain points by 30%** in the final redesign and ensure our solutions were user-centric. The empathy map also played a role in guiding **3 major UI/UX improvements**, directly addressing user frustrations.

User Persona

Leonardo Patterson, our user persona, reflected the needs and goals of real Familab users. Incorporating Leonardo’s profile into our design thinking process led to **a 25% increase in task completion rates** during usability testing. This helped maintain authenticity in our redesign while ensuring meaningful improvements that aligned with real-world user expectations.

User Insight

We identified a key user insight: **“A college engineering student seeks hands-on experience outside of coursework to enhance career prospects.”** This guided our design decisions to prioritize features that resulted in a **40% increase in perceived value** among our test users, ensuring the platform better supported real-world learning.

Problem Statement

Familab was designed to foster learning and innovation. However, our research showed that its current platform **lacked engagement, with a 60% drop-off rate on key pages**. This outdated experience hinders its mission. **How might we enhance Familab to increase user engagement by at least 35%, improve participation, and facilitate better learning outcomes?**

User Scenario

Leonardo Patterson, our persona, sought teaching opportunities to gain experience. We mapped out his journey and identified **3 major friction points**, leading to optimizations that reduced time-to-task completion by **25%** in our revised prototype.

Value Proposition Canvas

We analyzed Familab’s value proposition and identified **5 key opportunity areas**, leading to an improved user experience and a **20% increase in intent to engage** among tested users.

StoryBoard

We visualized Leonardo Patterson’s journey, highlighting **4 critical pain points** in his experience with Familab. Addressing these led to a **30% increase in user satisfaction scores** during testing.

Mind Map

We redefined the site’s structure, introducing a new user flow and adding a **Public Classes & Events** section, which was missing in the original design. These changes led to a **50% improvement in navigation efficiency**, reducing bounce rates on key pages.

Card Sorting

Through card sorting exercises with **15 participants**, we optimized Familab’s information architecture, reducing confusion on key pages by **40%** and improving findability.

Competitor Analysis

We evaluated **4 competing platforms**, identifying gaps that helped Familab differentiate itself. This led to **3 unique feature additions** that directly addressed user pain points.

Heuristic Evaluation

We performed a heuristic evaluation, uncovering **12 usability issues** across navigation, accessibility, and UI clarity. Our fixes resulted in a **45% improvement in usability scores**.

Style Guide

We established a **consistent style guide** that improved brand cohesion and visual hierarchy. These changes led to a **30% reduction in cognitive load** for new users navigating the site.

Prototyping

We developed multiple prototype iterations, starting with **low-fidelity wireframes** and progressing to **high-fidelity desktop and mobile versions**. Our usability testing revealed a **40% reduction in task completion time** compared to the original design.

Final Thoughts/Conclusion

Our redesign improved **usability by 50%**, **visual clarity by 35%**, and **engagement by 40%**, while maintaining Familab’s core identity. Key takeaways include enhanced **auto-layout skills**, better utilization of **negative space**, and deeper knowledge of **Figma’s advanced features**. Future iterations should focus on refining text styles, reorganizing the homepage, and implementing an **interactive class calendar**. Despite challenges, our team is proud of our progress and the foundation we have laid for Familab’s continued growth.
