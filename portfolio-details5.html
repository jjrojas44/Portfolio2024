Universal Studios website redesign

A redesign of the Universal Studios website.

Case Study:

Universal Studios website redesign
Project Overview
  • Project Name: Universal Studios Website Redesign
  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Solo Designer)
  • Tools Used: Figma, Adobe XD, Photoshop, Google Drive/Docs, Coolors
  • Timeline: 5 Weeks
  • Objective: Enhance usability, navigation, and booking experience while modernizing the website's design for a seamless user journey.
Value Added

A UX/UI redesign of the Universal Orlando website could elevate the user experience, making navigation seamless and intuitive without overwhelming visitors with text. By prioritizing visual cues and smooth interactions, users can easily find what they're looking for, guiding them through their journey effortlessly.

Problem Statement

Challenges with the Existing Website:

  • Confusing Booking Process: Users struggle to find ticket options and complete purchases.
  • Overwhelming Navigation: Too many menu items create frustration.
  • Outdated Visual Design: Poor UI hierarchy and inconsistent branding.
  • Lack of Mobile Optimization: The mobile experience is clunky and difficult to use.

Why a Redesign?

  • To provide a modern, streamlined experience that enhances usability and increases conversions.
  • To improve user satisfaction with an intuitive, visually engaging website.
  • To create a more immersive on-brand experience, aligning with Universal Studios' entertainment focus.
Research & User Insights
Persona
User Insight

Emily Carter, a 34-year-old marketing manager from Orlando, is highly detail-oriented and expects a seamless, visually engaging, and information-rich digital experience. As a mother, she values efficiency, clear navigation, and well-structured content when planning trips for her family. However, she finds the current Universal Studios website overwhelming and difficult to navigate, making trip planning more frustrating than exciting.
