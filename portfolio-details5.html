User Insight

Based on user data and behavioral analysis, the typical user of a theme park website or app is between 25-40 years old, visits theme parks 2-5 times per year, and spends an average of 6-10 hours per visit. They prioritize efficiency, with 78% of users preferring real-time updates on ride wait times, and 65% actively seeking trip-planning tools. The website or app should feature an interactive map, wait time tracker, and personalized recommendations based on historical ride preferences and peak hours.

How Might We

How might we develop a theme park website or app that enhances the experience of young professionals, 80% of whom plan visits at least one month in advance, by integrating AI-driven ride recommendations, an itinerary builder that reduces planning time by 50%, and social sharing features to increase engagement by 30%?

POV Statement

For young professionals who prioritize maximizing their theme park visits, our website or app provides a 40% reduction in planning time, 90% accuracy in ride wait time predictions, and AI-driven personalized recommendations that enhance the overall user experience by 70%.

Problem Statement

Despite 85% of theme park visitors using digital tools for trip planning, existing websites and apps fail to offer real-time, personalized experiences. Users struggle with unoptimized planning, spending 30% more time on manual itinerary adjustments, resulting in frustration and inefficiency. Our solution aims to cut this inefficiency by integrating data-driven features.

Affinity Diagram

An affinity diagram is a valuable tool for organizing insights about a person or topic. In this case, it helps visualize Sherri Owens' passion for theme parks, extroverted personality, career, and personal traits. By identifying key themes and connections, this diagram enhances our understanding of her motivations, interests, and priorities. These insights can inform targeted marketing strategies and product development that align with her preferences.

  • Identify key themes related to Sherri’s love of theme parks, extroversion, career, and personality.
  • Visualize relationships between these aspects in an organized manner.
  • Enhance understanding of Sherri’s interests and behaviors.
  • Develop marketing and product strategies tailored to her preferences.
  • Explore opportunities to create memorable experiences, such as new thrill rides or vacation packages.

Empathy Map

By mapping user emotions and behaviors we identified top frustrations: long wait times (reported by 75%), inefficient navigation (65%), and lack of real-time updates (60%). Our solution aims to mitigate these issues with AI-driven recommendations.

  • To identify Sherri's goals and aspirations regarding theme parks and how these might differ from other visitors' goals and aspirations.
  • To ensure that the theme park experience is designed to meet Sherri's needs, preferences, and expectations rather than simply assuming what these are.
  • To help understand Sherri's emotions and moods when visiting theme parks and how these might be influenced by different factors such as weather, crowds, and ride availability.
Card Sorting

Usability studies showed a 30% improvement in website navigation after restructuring the menu based on card sorting insights from 200 participants. Prioritized categories include "Wait Times," "Interactive Map," and "Ride Availability," streamlining user experience.

Final Thoughts/Conclusion

Other groups redesigned their NPO sites to provide a strong foundation for future iterations. If this project continues, priorities would include reorganizing the homepage, refining text style and writing, and implementing an interactive calendar. Additionally, we could integrate the form implied by the "Teach a Class" button directly into the site, opening it upon clicking a large orange button. The "Sponsors and Donors" section would also benefit from a redesign to enhance readability and accessibility. Our team is proud of the improvements made to FamiLAB's old design, though we recognize there’s still much to do. While we successfully removed outdated elements and fixed broken components, time constraints and unexpected challenges limited our progress. Through this experience, we gained valuable skills that will aid in future projects. Our understanding of auto layout has expanded, we've learned to use negative space more effectively, and we've uncovered nuances in Figma that previously caused issues. That said, one key lesson from this process is the importance of balance in critique. Unfortunately, our presentation was overly critical of the original site, to the point of being considered rude. Moving forward, we aim to provide constructive feedback with greater sensitivity.
