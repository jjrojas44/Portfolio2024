Case Study:

Universal Studios Website Redesign
Project Overview
  • Project Name: Universal Studios Website Redesign
  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Solo Designer)
  • Tools Used: Figma, Adobe XD, Photoshop, Google Drive/Docs, Coolors
  • Timeline: 5 Weeks
  • Objective: Enhance usability, navigation, and booking experience while modernizing the website's design for a seamless user journey.
Value Added

The Universal Orlando website would benefit from a UX/UI redesign that creates an improved user experience through simple navigation while avoiding excessive text. The combination of visual cues and smooth interactions enables users to locate information easily, leading them through their journey without difficulty.

Problem Statement

Guests visiting the Universal Studios website struggle with a complex and outdated interface that makes it difficult to book tickets, find information, and navigate the site, especially on mobile devices. This negatively impacts user satisfaction and leads to high abandonment rates during critical booking and planning stages.

Identified Issues with the Current Universal Studios Website:

  • Confusing Booking Process: Guests struggled to locate ticketing options and complete purchases efficiently, contributing to elevated cart abandonment rates.
  • Overwhelming Navigation: Excessive menu items and poor content hierarchy made it difficult for users to quickly find information, increasing frustration and session drop-offs.
  • Outdated Visual Design: Inconsistent branding, cluttered visuals, and weak visual hierarchy undermined the site’s credibility and hindered ease of use.
  • Inconsistent Web Responsiveness: The website’s layout and interface elements failed to adapt reliably across different screen sizes, with small tap targets, overlapping content, and scaling issues that disrupted the booking and planning experience on mobile browsers.

Why a Redesign?

  • To provide a modern, guest-focused experience that minimizes user frustration and improves ticket purchase conversions by simplifying key booking flows.
  • To enhance satisfaction and engagement by delivering an intuitive, clean, and visually appealing website that adheres to current UX/UI design standards.
  • To build a consistent, immersive digital experience that reflects Universal Studios’ dynamic brand while ensuring seamless usability across all device types and screen sizes.

Research

Persona

User Insight

Emily Carter, a 34-year-old marketing manager from Orlando, values clear, intuitive digital experiences. As both a professional and a mother, she expects seamless, visually engaging, and information-rich platforms that save her time and minimize frustration while planning family outings. This persona guided every design decision, ensuring functionality and aesthetics catered directly to users like Emily.

How Might We

  • Create a streamlined and visually intuitive Universal Studios website that simplifies trip planning for detail-oriented, family-focused users like Emily.
  • Revamp the booking process to be clear and efficient, minimizing cognitive load and reducing the number of steps needed to secure tickets and plan visits.
  • Offer a personalized, relevant experience that addresses both professional expectations for high usability and personal needs for ease of use, especially on mobile devices.

POV Statement

As a marketing manager and mother, Emily Carter needs an intuitive, streamlined Universal Studios website because the existing platform is overwhelming, difficult to navigate, and poorly optimized for mobile — making trip planning unnecessarily stressful. This redesign aims to resolve these issues by prioritizing clarity, modern UI design, and device responsiveness.

Competitve Analysis

Competitive Takeaways

  • Walt Disney World: Heavy emphasis on immersive visuals and brand storytelling, but important booking actions like ticket purchases and hotel reservations are buried within multiple nested menus. This created delays for users wanting quick access to functional tools.
  • SeaWorld: Prioritized clear, front-and-center functionality, making ticket and park info easy to find. However, the site lacked brand personality and visually felt generic compared to competitors, reducing overall engagement.
  • LEGOLAND: Strong, playful branding and consistent visuals made the experience feel welcoming, but the navigation felt cluttered, especially on mobile. Users had to dig through several categories to reach ticket booking and seasonal event pages.
  • Six Flags: Focused heavily on promotions and upsells, which led to an overwhelming homepage filled with banners and pop-ups. This created cognitive overload and delayed access to core functions like park hours and ride information.

Biggest Challenge

  • The original Universal Studios website presented a cluttered, outdated layout with low contrast and competing visual elements that made navigation confusing and overwhelming.
  • Key actions like purchasing tickets, viewing park hours, or accessing maps were not clearly emphasized, causing users to feel lost in a sea of links and banners.
  • The homepage lacked a strong visual hierarchy, resulting in poor readability and scattered user attention.
  • My challenge was to redesign the site with a modern, intuitive layout while preserving the general structure and familiarity that returning guests expect.
  • Through several iterations, I introduced bold calls-to-action, improved spacing, streamlined content sections, and incorporated immersive visuals—enhancing both usability and the emotional excitement of the Universal brand.

Key Insights

  • The current Universal Studios website prioritizes high-impact visuals and promotions over functional usability, making it difficult for users to complete key planning actions like booking tickets or accessing park details.
  • Navigation is overwhelming and poorly structured, with excessive menu items and unclear hierarchy that cause confusion and increase user drop-off rates.
  • The outdated visual design lacks cohesion and credibility, making the site feel less trustworthy and harder to use compared to modern standards.
  • There is a major missed opportunity to create a sense of magic and personalization that aligns with Universal Studios’ immersive brand experience.
  • Critical booking flows are buried or inconsistent, leading to user frustration and increased cart abandonment during the planning and checkout process.
  • Key utility areas like the footer and ticketing sections feel cluttered and disorganized, detracting from the overall professionalism and efficiency of the site.

UI Style Guide

My Design Process

  • Competitive & Visual Analysis: I began by analyzing competitor sites like Disney, Six Flags, and SeaWorld to better understand modern design standards and what Universal’s current website was lacking in comparison.
  • Identifying Core Problems: Through my own review of Universal’s website, I identified pain points such as cluttered layouts, inconsistent visual hierarchy, confusing navigation, and underwhelming user flow for key actions like buying tickets.
  • Defining the Experience: Without user interviews, I imagined myself as a typical guest (parent, tourist, fan) to define what an ideal experience should feel like: clear, exciting, quick, and easy to navigate from homepage to checkout.
  • Wireframing (Low-Fidelity): I sketched out structural improvements using wireframes, focusing on simplifying the layout, removing distractions, and guiding users toward major calls to action like “Buy Tickets” and “Plan Your Visit.”
  • UI Design & Style Development: I created a custom style guide based on Universal’s existing branding, enhancing it with better contrast, modern typography, and engaging visuals. I aimed for a theme-park vibe that felt clean and bold.
  • High-Fidelity Mockups: Using Figma, I translated my concepts into polished, interactive high-fidelity screens. All design decisions were driven by my own critique of the existing design and best practices in modern UX/UI.
  • Visual Refinement: I refined spacing, typography, and CTA prominence through design iterations, relying on personal UX/UI knowledge to push the design toward a more professional and immersive presentation.

Key Design Decisions

As the project evolved, I made several key adjustments to improve usability, enhance guest excitement, and align the site with Universal’s immersive brand experience:

  • Added a full-featured hamburger menu with a clear, organized list of options including Theme Park Portal, Universal Immersive Experiences, and direct links to Settings, Help, Language Selection, Contact, and a simplified Login method to improve account access.
  • Renamed primary navigation categories to more fun, theme-driven labels that add personality and excitement to the guest experience while retaining clarity and intuitive wayfinding.
  • Replaced static hero images with dynamic, tastefully themed imagery and sneak peek visuals of attractions, giving guests an immediate sense of the Universal Studios atmosphere as they browse.
  • Completely reorganized the site footer, which was previously cluttered and disordered, into a clean, structured layout with grouped links, social icons, and a consistent All Rights Reserved statement for a polished, professional finish.
  • Adjusted color distribution across UI elements to improve visual hierarchy, ensuring CTAs, alerts, and essential information stood out while maintaining a cohesive brand look and feel.

What I Learned

  • Balancing Visual Appeal and Usability: Creating a visually engaging website while maintaining clear navigation and easy user flows is crucial for a successful web design.
  • Conducting Self-Guided Research: When direct user feedback isn’t available, using personas, competitor analysis, and personal observations helps identify user needs and pain points.
  • Iterative Design Process: Refining wireframes and mockups through multiple iterations improves visual hierarchy, spacing, and the prominence of key calls to action.
  • Reflecting Brand Identity in UI: Translating the Universal Studios brand into a modern, clean web design that is both professional and playful enhances user engagement and trust.
  • Improving Content Structure: Simplifying navigation and organizing content clearly helps users find important information quickly and efficiently.
