Case Study:

Universal Studios website redesign
Project Overview
  • Project Name: Universal Studios Website Redesign
  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Solo Designer)
  • Tools Used: Figma, Adobe XD, Photoshop, Google Drive/Docs, Coolors
  • Timeline: 5 Weeks
  • Objective: Enhance usability, navigation, and booking experience while modernizing the website's design for a seamless user journey.
Value Added

A UX/UI redesign of the Universal Orlando website could elevate the user experience, making navigation seamless and intuitive without overwhelming visitors with text. By prioritizing visual cues and smooth interactions, users can easily find what they're looking for, guiding them through their journey effortlessly.

Problem Statement

Challenges with Halo Infinite:

  • Lack of Mission Replayability: Players couldn't replay campaign missions at launch.
  • Unfinished Live-Service Model: Delayed content and lack of engaging progression.
  • Limited Biome Diversity: The open world felt repetitive, lacking distinct locations.
  • Multiplayer Progression Issues: Battle Pass felt slow and unrewarding.

Why a Sequel?

  • To refine the open-world design with more diverse environments.
  • To implement a more engaging and rewarding progression system.
  • To expand on Halo’s multiplayer and co-op experience.
  • To deepen the narrative with stronger character development and emotional stakes.
Research & User Insights
Persona
User Insight

Alex Carter is a dedicated Halo fan and competitive gamer who values engaging progression, immersive storytelling, and strategic gameplay. However, he feels frustrated by the lack of mission replayability, slow Battle Pass progression, and an unfinished live-service model in Halo Infinite. He wishes for a sequel that refines these mechanics, making the experience more rewarding, diverse, and immersive.
