The UI for a Halo Infinite sequel, Halo Infinite: Requiem.

Case Study:

Halo Infinite Requiem
Project Overview
  • Project Name: Halo Infinite Requiem (Hypothetical Sequel)
  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Solo Designer)
  • Tools Used: Figma,Photoshop,Illustrator,Gameuidatabase,dribbble,Behance,
  • Timeline: 2 Weeks
  • Objective: Expand on Halo Infinite’s foundation by refining gameplay mechanics, improving open-world engagement, and deepening the story. Introduce more biome diversity, richer NPC interactions, and enhanced AI for a more immersive Halo experience.
Value Added

Halo Infinite Requiem serves as a sequel to Halo Infinite—Halo Infinite Requiem—which expands on the first game while incorporating feedback from the community. The experience would become more dynamic and engaging through UI placement improvements and fixes to menu navigation, layout, and multiplayer variety.

Problem Statement

The current UI design and user experience in Halo Infinite presents several pain points that diminish long-term player engagement and satisfaction. From a confusing progression system to repetitive menus and lackluster multiplayer navigation, the game struggles to deliver a modern, accessible, and rewarding experience expected from a flagship title in the franchise.

Challenges with the Existing UI from Halo: Infinite:

  • Limited Replayability: Players lack the ability to easily replay campaign missions, reducing long-term single-player value.
  • Overcomplicated Progression: Battle Pass systems feel slow and unrewarding, leading to frustration and drop-offs among competitive players.
  • Outdated Visual Hierarchy: Inconsistent UI patterns, clunky navigation, and visual clutter create confusion and detract from immersion.
  • Weak Customization Experience: Accessing unlockables and personalization options requires navigating a bloated, unintuitive menu system.

Why a Design for a Hypothetical Sequel?

  • To deliver a fresh, streamlined, and modernized Halo experience that respects franchise traditions while embracing contemporary UX/UI design principles.
  • To increase player satisfaction and retention by refining key systems like multiplayer navigation, mission selection, and progression feedback loops.
  • To build a visually cohesive and emotionally engaging experience that unifies story, UI, and player agency in ways that enhance both competitive and narrative-driven gameplay.

Research

Persona

User Insight

Alex Carter is a dedicated Halo fan and competitive gamer who values engaging progression systems, immersive narratives, and dynamic multiplayer experiences. He’s frustrated by Halo Infinite’s unfinished features, grind-heavy systems, and limited mission replayability, and seeks a sequel that feels complete, rewarding, and emotionally resonant.

How Might We

  • Design a more rewarding progression system with meaningful, skill-based unlocks and clear milestone markers to keep Alex engaged.
  • Expand open-world exploration with new, diverse biomes, interactive environments, and spontaneous emergent events to reduce repetitive gameplay loops.
  • Improve multiplayer community features with streamlined matchmaking, cooperative mission options, and accessible social tools to foster collaboration and replay value.
  • Deepen narrative impact with emotionally-driven character arcs, replayable missions, and choice-based outcomes that affect the game world and its inhabitants.

POV Statement

As a competitive and lore-driven gamer, Alex Carter wants a Halo sequel that offers richer mission replayability, faster progression systems, and a visually coherent, emotionally resonant world because he wants to stay consistently challenged and emotionally invested without enduring the grind or feature gaps of Halo Infinite.

Competitive Analysis

UI Improvements

    Improved HUD and Accessibility: Introduced a customizable UI to allow players to adjust visual clarity, scale HUD elements, and personalize gameplay visibility. This increases accessibility for a wider range of players without sacrificing the immersive Halo aesthetic.

    Enhanced Navigation: Reimagined core menu navigation by returning to a simplified, back-to-basics system where major categories are easily accessible, and submenus are logically nested. This reduces player frustration and menu fatigue.

    Dynamic Multiplayer UI: Combined the clean, readable design of Halo Reach with modern matchmaking tools and stat-tracking inspired by Halo 5, creating a familiar but updated experience for competitive and social players alike.

    Store Improvement: Streamlined the in-game store UI to prioritize item visuals, prices, and clear item descriptions. Created a categorized layout with improved discoverability and a cleaner checkout flow to reduce friction and boost engagement.

  • Customization Overhaul:
    • Simplified the customization interface by consolidating multiple menus into one unified screen, improving discoverability and reducing unnecessary steps.
    • Introduced one-to-two screen pathways for customizing loadouts, armor, and Spartan ID, ensuring clarity and efficiency.
    • Allowed players to name their Spartans and create personalized class presets for faster loadout switching between campaign and multiplayer.
    • Created a unified inventory area for battle pass, store, and unlockable items to minimize menu hunting and improve engagement with acquired content.
