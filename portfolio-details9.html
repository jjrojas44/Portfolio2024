Halo Infinte Requiem

The UI for a Halo Infinite sequel, Halo Infinite: Requiem.

Case Study:

Halo Infinite Requiem
Project Overview
  • Project Name: Halo Infinite Requiem (Hypothetical Sequel)
  • Role: UX/UI Designer (Solo Designer)
  • Tools Used: Figma,Photoshop,Illustrator,Gameuidatabase,dribbble,Behance,
  • Timeline: 2 Weeks
  • Objective: Expand on Halo Infinite’s foundation by refining gameplay mechanics, improving open-world engagement, and deepening the story. Introduce more biome diversity, richer NPC interactions, and enhanced AI for a more immersive Halo experience.
Value Added

Halo Infinite Requiem serves as a sequel to Halo Infinite—Halo Infinite Requiem—which expands on the first game while incorporating feedback from the community. The experience would become more dynamic and engaging through UI placement improvements and fixes to menu navigation, layout, and multiplayer variety.

Problem Statement

The current UI design and user experience in Halo Infinite presents several pain points that diminish long-term player engagement and satisfaction. From a confusing progression system to repetitive menus and lackluster multiplayer navigation, the game struggles to deliver a modern, accessible, and rewarding experience expected from a flagship title in the franchise.

Challenges with the Existing UI from Halo: Infinite:

  • Limited Replayability: Players lack the ability to easily replay campaign missions, reducing long-term single-player value.
  • Overcomplicated Progression: Battle Pass systems feel slow and unrewarding, leading to frustration and drop-offs among competitive players.
  • Outdated Visual Hierarchy: Inconsistent UI patterns, clunky navigation, and visual clutter create confusion and detract from immersion.
  • Weak Customization Experience: Accessing unlockables and personalization options requires navigating a bloated, unintuitive menu system.

Why a Design for a Hypothetical Sequel?

  • To deliver a fresh, streamlined, and modernized Halo experience that respects franchise traditions while embracing contemporary UX/UI design principles.
  • To increase player satisfaction and retention by refining key systems like multiplayer navigation, mission selection, and progression feedback loops.
  • To build a visually cohesive and emotionally engaging experience that unifies story, UI, and player agency in ways that enhance both competitive and narrative-driven gameplay.

Research

Persona

User Insight

For Alex, Halo should be about adrenaline-pumping multiplayer, memorable story beats, and clear rewards for skill. Like many players, he feels let down by Infinite’s unfinished state and tedious unlock systems. I focused my redesign around giving players like Alex the features and pacing they expected from the start.

Key Goals

  • Build a progression system that feels earned, with rewards tied to personal skill and clear in-game milestones.
  • Break up open-world repetition by adding dynamic world events, multiple environments, and replayable missions with meaningful differences.
  • Make online multiplayer feel social again, with smarter matchmaking filters, better squad tools, and easier custom game options.
  • Strengthen the story’s emotional weight by integrating more character-driven missions and player-choice outcomes that leave a lasting mark.

POV Statement

As a competitive, lore-loving Halo veteran, Alex Carter wants a sequel that finally delivers the lasting multiplayer, rich campaign, and rewarding unlock system he was promised with Infinite — because without those, even a solid shooter becomes forgettable in a crowded market.

Empathy Map

To better capture the player mindset, I mapped out what Alex sees, hears, thinks, and feels during his typical Halo sessions. This made it easier to identify where frustrations build and what features could turn those moments into something positive.

Competitive Analysis

I compared Halo Infinite’s systems to other popular competitive shooters like Rainbow Six Siege, Destiny 2, Call of Duty: Black Ops 6, and Battlefield 2042. These games highlighted what Halo was lacking — robust progression systems, dependable social features, and player-driven customization. Those insights directly shaped several of my redesign concepts.

Competitive Takeaways

  • Rainbow Six Siege: Excels at deep progression and customization systems that reward skillful play, but its steep learning curve can alienate new players.
  • Destiny 2: Offers a rich narrative and rewarding loot systems that drive long-term engagement, though its content can feel grind-heavy at times.
  • Call of Duty: Black Ops 6: Prioritizes fast matchmaking and accessible gameplay loops, but its menu design often sacrifices depth and personalization for speed.
  • Battlefield 2042: Known for large-scale, dynamic multiplayer battles and immersive environments, yet struggles with UI clutter and inconsistent feedback for players.

Biggest Challenge

  • The original Halo Infinite UI felt fragmented and outdated, with bloated menus that buried core features such as mission replay, customization, and multiplayer filters.
  • Progression systems lacked transparency and felt unrewarding, discouraging players from investing time in long-term unlocks.
  • Players had difficulty finding essential features quickly during gameplay, which disrupted flow and immersion.
  • My challenge was to streamline Halo’s complex systems into a cohesive experience while staying true to the franchise’s identity and meeting the expectations of both competitive and casual players.
  • Through iterative wireframes and high-fidelity mockups, I unified the UI design, improved navigation, and prioritized clarity and feedback in all player interactions.

Key Insights

  • Modern competitive shooters reward players with clear progression and personalization systems; Halo Infinite’s original approach felt underdeveloped and inconsistent.
  • Players want frictionless access to core actions like matchmaking, squad creation, and customization, especially in the heat of multiplayer matches.
  • Clarity and readability in UI elements (colors, typography, iconography) directly affect player confidence and enjoyment during intense sessions.
  • Integrating campaign, multiplayer, and customization into a unified, logical structure helps players transition seamlessly between modes.
  • Emphasizing customization and social tools fosters stronger community engagement, a proven driver of player retention in competing titles.
  • Balancing franchise nostalgia with modern UI/UX practices ensures Halo Requiem appeals to veterans while welcoming new players.

UI & Gameplay Experience Improvements

Based on what Alex — and Halo’s wider audience — has repeatedly asked for, here’s what I prioritized in this redesign:

  • Customizable HUD & Accessibility: Give players control over what clutters their screen and how big or bright it appears. It makes intense firefights cleaner and helps more players feel comfortable jumping in.
  • Streamlined Menus: Halo’s menus have gotten bloated. I brought it back to basics, putting essential categories upfront while organizing the rest in simple, logical submenus players can actually find mid-match.
  • Modernized Matchmaking & Social Tools: Borrowing the clean readability of Halo Reach’s UI with modern filters and custom lobby tools from titles like Apex, this design makes it faster to find good matches or assemble a fireteam.
  • Store Redesign: Infinite’s current store buries items in tedious scrolling. I reorganized it by category, prioritized visuals and prices, and added quick filters to make finding what you want effortless.
  • Armor & Loadout Customization Overhaul: I merged multiple menus into one clean customization hub, cutting down steps to swap armor, adjust loadouts, and equip cosmetics. I also added the option to save class presets for campaign and multiplayer separately.

My Design Process

  • Competitive & Visual Analysis: I analyzed popular shooters such as Rainbow Six Siege, Destiny 2, Call of Duty: Black Ops 6, and Battlefield 2042 to understand how they handled progression systems, social features, and customization. This revealed opportunities where Halo Infinite could modernize without losing its unique identity.
  • Identifying Core Problems: I reviewed Halo Infinite’s existing UI and community feedback to pinpoint frustrations like bloated menus, slow navigation, weak visual hierarchy, and an unrewarding progression system.
  • Defining the Experience: Without direct user interviews, I envisioned the needs of competitive and casual Halo players, focusing on clear feedback, fast access to multiplayer tools, and streamlined customization and mission replay options.
  • Wireframing (Low-Fidelity): I sketched new menu layouts and system flows, removing unnecessary layers and simplifying navigation to prioritize essential features such as matchmaking, customization, and mission selection.
  • UI Design & Style Development: I developed a cohesive visual style aligned with Halo’s sci-fi theme, using bold typography, immersive backgrounds, and clean iconography to create a modernized yet familiar experience.
  • High-Fidelity Mockups: Using Figma, I translated the wireframes into high-fidelity screens. I restructured the menus and incorporated visual feedback for progression, improved customization hubs, and clearer matchmaking filters.
  • Visual Refinement: Through several iterations, I fine-tuned visual hierarchy, refined the color palette, and adjusted spacing to ensure players could navigate quickly during high-pressure multiplayer sessions while maintaining an immersive presentation.

Wireframe & Design Evolution

High Fidelity

After validating the wireframe structure, I transitioned to high-fidelity designs to enhance visual hierarchy, improve call-to-action clarity, and align the UI with the modern sci-fi aesthetic of the Halo universe while addressing player pain points identified in competitor analysis.

Iteration: First Iteration vs. Final Iteration

Between the first and final iterations, I addressed issues with color scheme consistency, integrating thematic, sci-fi-inspired hues to enhance visual flow and screen balance. I improved weak button visibility, refined the background image to better serve as an immersive, in-game main menu backdrop, and selected new typography that aligns more closely with the futuristic aesthetic of the Halo universe. These adjustments created a more cohesive, readable, and engaging experience for players navigating the UI.

What I Learned

  • Designing for Complex Systems: I learned how important it is to simplify layered game systems (progression, customization, matchmaking) without removing depth for players.
  • Adapting Competitor Insights: Analyzing games like Rainbow Six Siege, Destiny 2, and Call of Duty showed me how different approaches to progression and UI structure can inspire Halo’s unique solution without copying competitors.
  • Iterative UI Refinement: Multiple wireframe and mockup iterations were critical to creating cleaner navigation, better menu organization, and a more cohesive user flow.
  • Balancing Nostalgia with Modern UX: Retaining Halo’s legacy design elements while updating visuals and interactions for modern players was key to user trust and engagement.
  • Clarity in High-Stakes Contexts: I learned how crucial clear visual hierarchy, responsive feedback, and fast navigation are during competitive multiplayer experiences where every second counts.
