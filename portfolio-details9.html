Problem Statement

The current UI design and user experience in Halo Infinite presents several pain points that diminish long-term player engagement and satisfaction. From a confusing progression system to repetitive menus and lackluster multiplayer navigation, the game struggles to deliver a modern, accessible, and rewarding experience expected from a flagship title in the franchise.

Challenges with the Existing UI from Halo: Infinite:

  • Limited Replayability: Players lack the ability to easily replay campaign missions, reducing long-term single-player value.
  • Overcomplicated Progression: Battle Pass systems feel slow and unrewarding, leading to frustration and drop-offs among competitive players.
  • Outdated Visual Hierarchy: Inconsistent UI patterns, clunky navigation, and visual clutter create confusion and detract from immersion.
  • Weak Customization Experience: Accessing unlockables and personalization options requires navigating a bloated, unintuitive menu system.

Why a Design for a Hypothetical Sequel?

  • To deliver a fresh, streamlined, and modernized Halo experience that respects franchise traditions while embracing contemporary UX/UI design principles.
  • To increase player satisfaction and retention by refining key systems like multiplayer navigation, mission selection, and progression feedback loops.
  • To build a visually cohesive and emotionally engaging experience that unifies story, UI, and player agency in ways that enhance both competitive and narrative-driven gameplay.

Competitive Analysis

Benchmarking top shooters highlighted three gaps Halo must close: progression clarity, social tools, and customization flow.

Wireframe & Design Evolution

UI & Gameplay Experience Improvements

Based on what Alex — and Halo’s wider audience — has repeatedly asked for, here’s what I prioritized in this redesign:

  • Customizable HUD & Accessibility: Give players control over what clutters their screen and how big or bright it appears. It makes intense firefights cleaner and helps more players feel comfortable jumping in.
  • Streamlined Menus: Halo’s menus have gotten bloated. I brought it back to basics, putting essential categories upfront while organizing the rest in simple, logical submenus players can actually find mid-match.
  • Modernized Matchmaking & Social Tools: Borrowing the clean readability of Halo Reach’s UI with modern filters and custom lobby tools from titles like Apex, this design makes it faster to find good matches or assemble a fireteam.
  • Store Redesign: Infinite’s current store buries items in tedious scrolling. I reorganized it by category, prioritized visuals and prices, and added quick filters to make finding what you want effortless.
  • Armor & Loadout Customization Overhaul: I merged multiple menus into one clean customization hub, cutting down steps to swap armor, adjust loadouts, and equip cosmetics. I also added the option to save class presets for campaign and multiplayer separately.

High Fidelity

After validating the wireframe structure, I transitioned to high-fidelity designs to enhance visual hierarchy, improve call-to-action clarity, and align the UI with the modern sci-fi aesthetic of the Halo universe while addressing player pain points identified in competitor analysis.

Iteration: First Iteration vs. Final Iteration

Between the first and final iterations, I addressed issues with color scheme consistency, integrating thematic, sci-fi-inspired hues to enhance visual flow and screen balance. I improved weak button visibility, refined the background image to better serve as an immersive, in-game main menu backdrop, and selected new typography that aligns more closely with the futuristic aesthetic of the Halo universe. These adjustments created a more cohesive, readable, and engaging experience for players navigating the UI.

What I Learned

  • Designing for Complex Systems: I learned how important it is to simplify layered game systems (progression, customization, matchmaking) without removing depth for players.
  • Adapting Competitor Insights: Analyzing games like Rainbow Six Siege, Destiny 2, and Call of Duty showed me how different approaches to progression and UI structure can inspire Halo’s unique solution without copying competitors.
  • Iterative UI Refinement: Multiple wireframe and mockup iterations were critical to creating cleaner navigation, better menu organization, and a more cohesive user flow.
  • Balancing Nostalgia with Modern UX: Retaining Halo’s legacy design elements while updating visuals and interactions for modern players was key to user trust and engagement.
  • Clarity in High-Stakes Contexts: I learned how crucial clear visual hierarchy, responsive feedback, and fast navigation are during competitive multiplayer experiences where every second counts.
